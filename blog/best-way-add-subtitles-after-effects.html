Best Way to Add Subtitles to After Effects Projects

There is no single "best" subtitle workflow for every project - but there is a best method for your deliverable. This guide compares manual captions, SRT imports, Premiere round-trips, and AI plugins so you can pick the right approach.

1. Manual text layers

Type each line, trim to audio, style individually. Zero extra cost, maximum tedium.

Choose this when: you have under 30 seconds of dialogue or need pixel-perfect control on three words.
Skip when: you're captioning interviews, podcasts, or anything over a minute.

2. SRT / VTT import

Transcribe elsewhere, import timed text. Good for accessibility deliverables; weak for motion-designed social captions.

Choose this when: you already have an SRT and only need basic on-screen text.
Skip when: you need karaoke highlights, brand fonts, or animated backgrounds.

3. Premiere Pro speech-to-text → After Effects

Premiere's auto-transcribe is solid for editorial. Export captions, import to AE, restyle.

Choose this when: your master timeline is in Premiere and AE is for a short motion section.
Skip when: the entire project lives in After Effects - the round-trip adds friction.

Which subtitle method should you choose?

Use this decision tree:

  • Short clip, few words? → Manual layers
  • Already have SRT, plain text OK? → Import SRT
  • Edit in Premiere, motion section in AE? → Premiere transcribe → import
  • Full AE workflow, styled social captions? → AI plugin in AE

For most motion designers posting to Instagram, TikTok, or YouTube Shorts, an in-AE AI plugin is the best balance of speed and creative control.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best way to add subtitles in After Effects?

For styled social and motion graphics work, an AI caption plugin inside After Effects is usually best - it combines automatic transcription with native text layers you can animate. For plain accessibility SRT delivery, import or export sidecar files.

Should I add subtitles in Premiere or After Effects?

Use Premiere if your final edit and delivery are editorial. Use After Effects when captions need custom fonts, motion, backgrounds, or word-level animation.

Can you import subtitles into After Effects?

Yes. SRT and VTT files can be imported via scripts or caption plugins. Imported subtitles provide timing but usually need restyling to match your motion design.
