How to Add Karaoke-Style Captions in After Effects

Karaoke-style captions highlight each word as it's spoken — the look viewers expect on TikTok, Reels, and Shorts. Here's how to build word-by-word subtitle animations in After Effects, manually or with AI.

What are karaoke-style captions?

Karaoke captions (also called word-highlight or bounce captions) display a full line of text but emphasise the word currently being spoken — usually with a colour change, scale pop, or background highlight.

Unlike static subtitles that show a whole sentence at once, karaoke captions:

  • Increase retention on muted autoplay feeds
  • Guide the viewer through fast dialogue
  • Look native to short-form social platforms

Manual method: separate layers per word

The pure AE approach splits every word onto its own text layer, then keyframes colour or opacity per word. Accurate but extremely time-consuming.

  1. Transcribe dialogue and note word timings (stopwatch against audio).
  2. Create one text layer per word.
  3. Set in/out points to match speech.
  4. Keyframing: active word = brand colour; inactive = grey.

Time estimate: 2–4 hours for a 60-second clip. Not practical for client work at volume.

Using After Effects text animators

AE's Range Selector and text animators can highlight characters when you have per-word timing data. You still need a source of word timestamps — either manual markup or imported JSON/SRT with word-level data.

This method suits experienced AE users who want custom expressions. Most editors prefer a plugin that handles timing automatically.

AI workflow: word-level transcription + highlight presets

The practical approach for karaoke captions in 2026:

  1. Transcribe with word timestamps — AI returns when each word starts and ends.
  2. Pick a highlight animation — spotlight, highlighter bar, or colour pop.
  3. Set colours — base text + active word highlight colour.
  4. Apply to timeline — plugin generates timed text layers in a subtitles precomp.

Frequently asked questions

How do you make karaoke subtitles in After Effects?

Karaoke subtitles require word-level timing: each word highlights as it is spoken. Use an AI caption tool with word timestamps and a highlight animation preset, or manually keyframe opacity or colour on separate text layers per word.

What are karaoke captions?

Karaoke captions show the current spoken word in a different colour or style while the rest of the line stays muted. They are standard on TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.

Can After Effects do word-by-word animation?

Yes. After Effects supports per-character and per-word text animators. Caption plugins automate this by combining word-level transcription timing with preset highlight animations.
